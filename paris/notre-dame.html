⛪ Notre-Dame de Paris

The restored cathedral anchors the Île de la Cité and remains one of the defining works of French Gothic architecture.

AREA

Parvis Notre-Dame, Île de la Cité

NEAREST TRANSPORT

Cité or Saint-Michel–Notre-Dame

ALLOW

45–90 minutes

Visit details

Opening hours

Open daily; generally 07:50–19:00 on weekdays and 08:15–19:30 at weekends. Services can pause visitor access.

How to get there

Take Métro line 4 to Cité, or RER B/C to Saint-Michel–Notre-Dame, then walk across the Île de la Cité.

Open in Google Maps

What to know

  • Circle the exterior to compare the west façade, flying buttresses and the quieter garden at the east end.
  • Inside, look for the vertical rhythm of the nave, rose windows and the contrast between medieval fabric and modern restoration.
  • The surrounding island offers excellent river views toward both banks.

Plan your visit

  • Cathedral entry is free, but visitor flow and optional reservations can change.
  • Dress and speak as you would in an active place of worship.
  • Visit early for a calmer interior and softer light on the façade.

Food & facilities nearby

Food nearby

Try the quieter streets around Rue d’Arcole or cross to the Latin Quarter rather than choosing the closest riverfront terraces.

Toilets nearby

Public toilets are available near the cathedral square and beside the Hôtel de Ville; opening can vary.

Photography & safety

Photography tips

Photograph the west façade from the parvis and the flying buttresses from Square Jean XXIII. Respect restrictions inside.

Safety tips

This is an active place of worship. Watch bags in queues and do not respond to petition approaches around the square.
