🏛️ Arc de Triomphe

A monumental endpoint to the Champs-Élysées with one of the clearest rooftop views of Paris's grand avenues.

AREA

Place Charles de Gaulle

NEAREST TRANSPORT

Charles de Gaulle–Étoile

ALLOW

60–90 minutes

Visit details

Opening hours

April–September: Wed–Mon 10:00–23:00, Tue 11:00–23:00. October–March closes at 22:30. Exceptional ceremonies can alter access.

How to get there

Take Métro lines 1, 2 or 6 to Charles de Gaulle–Étoile and use the signed pedestrian underpass. Never cross the roundabout.

Open in Google Maps

What to know

  • Use the pedestrian underpass; never try to cross the roundabout traffic.
  • At ground level, notice the sculpted groups and the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier beneath the arch.
  • From the terrace, the twelve radiating avenues make Haussmann's city plan immediately legible.

Plan your visit

  • There are many stairs before the terrace, with limited lift access for eligible visitors.
  • Late afternoon gives good light along the Champs-Élysées.
  • Check the official site for ceremony closures and current admission arrangements.

Food & facilities nearby

Food nearby

Avenues Wagram and Mac-Mahon have cafés away from the busiest Champs-Élysées frontage.

Toilets nearby

Toilets are inside the paid monument; larger cafés and the nearby Publicis complex have customer facilities.

Photography & safety

Photography tips

Use a wide lens beneath the arch and the terrace for the twelve radiating avenues. Late afternoon gives depth to the streets.

Safety tips

Use only the underpass. The terrace is exposed in poor weather and the surrounding pavements attract pickpocketing.
