City guides / Paris / The Seine

⛴ Seine Sightseeing Cruise

The river is Paris's best orientation line, linking monumental façades, bridges and the UNESCO-listed historic centre.

Booked: 1 July 2026 · Board at Pier Number 3
AREA

Central Paris riverbanks

NEAREST TRANSPORT

Multiple Métro stops on both banks

ALLOW

1 hour by boat or 2 hours walking

Visit details

Opening hours

The riverbanks are always accessible; cruise hours depend on operator and season. Confirm the booked Pier 3 departure directly.

How to get there

For Eiffel-area piers use Métro line 6 to Bir-Hakeim or line 9 to Trocadéro. Match the ticket’s exact operator and pier before leaving.

Open in Google Maps

What to know

  • A sightseeing boat compresses many landmarks into one view and reveals details hidden from street level.
  • On foot, the lower quays between the Eiffel Tower, Louvre and Île de la Cité make an easy self-guided route.
  • Notice how each bridge differs in age, decoration and relationship to the river.

Plan your visit

  • For a boat trip, confirm the operator, pier and latest sailing time directly.
  • Sit outside for views but carry a light layer; it can feel cooler on the water.
  • Late afternoon and blue hour give balanced light on both banks.

Food & facilities nearby

Food nearby

Kiosks and cafés are available around Port de la Bourdonnais and the Champ de Mars.

Toilets nearby

Operators normally provide toilets onboard; public facilities are also available around the Eiffel Tower esplanade.

Photography & safety

Photography tips

Choose an outside seat and a fast shutter for bridges. Late afternoon avoids harsh contrast between the banks.

Safety tips

River steps can be slippery. Check the boat name and pier, and keep phones secure rather than leaning over rails.
