🗼 Eiffel Tower

Paris's iron landmark rewards both a close look at its engineering and a wider view from across the Seine.

AREA

Champ de Mars, 7th arrondissement

NEAREST TRANSPORT

Bir-Hakeim, Trocadéro or École Militaire

ALLOW

2–3 hours with a tower visit

Visit details

Opening hours

Daily; date-specific hours vary, commonly from morning until late evening. Check the official calendar.

How to get there

Take Métro line 6 to Bir-Hakeim, line 9 to Trocadéro, or line 8 to École Militaire. Trocadéro gives the classic approach.

Open in Google Maps

What to know

  • Walk underneath to appreciate the scale and the latticework rather than treating it only as a skyline object.
  • For the classic full-height view, approach from Trocadéro; for a quieter foreground, try the Champ de Mars side.
  • The second level gives a detailed city view; the summit adds height but is more weather-dependent.

Plan your visit

  • Reserve tower tickets through the official site when a timed ascent matters.
  • Security queues are separate from lift or stair ticket queues.
  • After dark the tower sparkles briefly on the hour; keep belongings close in crowded viewpoints.

Food & facilities nearby

Food nearby

Cafés line Rue Saint-Dominique and Avenue de la Bourdonnais; tower venues require admission.

Toilets nearby

Public toilets are available on the tower esplanade and on each accessible tower level.

Photography & safety

Photography tips

Trocadéro works for the full tower; the Champ de Mars gives symmetry. Blue hour balances the lights and sky.

Safety tips

Use official entrances, expect security screening and secure valuables in the dense crowds around viewpoints.
