🎨 Montmartre

A steep, village-like quarter of stairways, studios and broad views above northern Paris.

AREA

18th arrondissement

NEAREST TRANSPORT

Anvers, Abbesses or Lamarck–Caulaincourt

ALLOW

2–4 hours on foot

Visit details

Opening hours

Neighbourhood streets are always open. Sacré-Cœur is generally open daily 06:30–22:30; dome access has separate seasonal hours.

How to get there

Take Métro line 2 to Anvers for the main approach, or line 12 to Abbesses. The funicular uses a standard local transport ticket.

Open in Google Maps

What to know

  • Start below Sacré-Cœur or arrive via Lamarck–Caulaincourt for a less direct, more atmospheric route.
  • Explore beyond Place du Tertre: Rue de l'Abreuvoir, the vineyard and quieter northern slopes show another side of the hill.
  • The basilica terrace gives a wide city view; the steps and funicular area are the busiest.

Plan your visit

  • Expect steep slopes, cobbles and stairs; the funicular avoids the main climb.
  • Go early in the morning for empty lanes and softer photographs.
  • Ignore bracelet sellers and keep valuables secure around dense crowds.

Food & facilities nearby

Food nearby

Move beyond Place du Tertre to Rue des Abbesses, Lamarck or Caulaincourt for less tourist-focused cafés.

Toilets nearby

Public toilets are near Square Louise Michel; cafés provide customer facilities. Availability on the hill is limited.

Photography & safety

Photography tips

Go early for empty lanes. Rue de l’Abreuvoir looks uphill; the Sacré-Cœur terrace gives the wide city view.

Safety tips

Expect steep cobbles. Ignore bracelet sellers below Sacré-Cœur and keep phones and wallets secure in bottlenecks.
