🎨 Louvre Museum

A former royal palace holding collections so vast that a selective plan is far better than trying to see everything.

AREA

Rue de Rivoli, 1st arrondissement

NEAREST TRANSPORT

Palais Royal–Musée du Louvre

ALLOW

2–4 hours for a focused visit

Visit details

Opening hours

Open 09:00–18:00; late opening to 21:00 on Wednesday and Friday. Closed Tuesday. Confirm holiday exceptions.

How to get there

Take Métro lines 1 or 7 to Palais Royal–Musée du Louvre; follow museum signs through the Carrousel or courtyard.

Open in Google Maps

What to know

  • Choose one wing or a short list of works before arriving; the building itself is part of the experience.
  • Beyond the Mona Lisa, the Winged Victory, the Apollo Gallery and the medieval foundations are memorable anchors.
  • Pause in the Cour Carrée and Tuileries to understand how the palace sits in the wider city axis.

Plan your visit

  • Use the official map and reserve a timed entry when required.
  • The pyramid is not the only entrance; follow the current instructions on the museum site.
  • Comfortable shoes matter, and large bags may need to be stored.

Food & facilities nearby

Food nearby

The museum has cafés; the Palais Royal and streets north of Rue de Rivoli offer more choice and quieter breaks.

Toilets nearby

Toilets are inside the museum after security and in the Carrousel du Louvre shopping area.

Photography & safety

Photography tips

The Cour Carrée is calmer for architecture; photograph the pyramid early or after dark. Follow gallery photography rules.

Safety tips

Reserve through the official museum, ignore ticket resellers near the entrances, and keep possessions closed in the busiest galleries.
