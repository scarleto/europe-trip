City guides / Amsterdam / Anne Frank House

📖 Anne Frank House

A restrained, powerful museum preserving the hiding place where Anne Frank wrote her diary during the Nazi occupation.

Booked: 4 July 2026 · 12:45–13:00 · Arrive 20–30 minutes early
AREA

Westermarkt 20

NEAREST TRANSPORT

Tram to Westermarkt or a 20-minute walk from Centraal

ALLOW

60–90 minutes

Visit details

Opening hours

Daily 09:00–22:00. Entry is only with an online timed ticket; check exceptional dates.

How to get there

Take tram 13 or 17 to Westermarkt, or walk about 20 minutes from Amsterdam Centraal.

Open in Google Maps

What to know

  • The route combines historical context, personal testimony and the unfurnished Secret Annex.
  • Move at the museum's quiet pace and leave time to absorb the final rooms rather than treating it as a checklist sight.
  • The surrounding Jordaan and Westerkerk help place the building in the living city Anne knew.

Plan your visit

  • Tickets are sold online for a specific time through the official museum only.
  • Arrive on time but do not queue excessively early on the narrow pavement.
  • Photography is not allowed inside; store the phone and focus on the visit.

Food & facilities nearby

Food nearby

The museum café is after the main route; nearby Westerstraat and Rozengracht offer more choice.

Toilets nearby

Visitor toilets are inside the museum; accessibility is limited in the historic annex.

Photography & safety

Photography tips

Photography is not allowed inside. Exterior photographs are easiest from across Prinsengracht without blocking the pavement.

Safety tips

Arrive for the booked slot, keep the narrow entrance clear and treat the site and other visitors respectfully.
