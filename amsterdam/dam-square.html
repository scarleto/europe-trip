City guides / Amsterdam / Dam Square

🏛️ Dam Square

Amsterdam's historic civic centre is busy and imperfect, but it remains the best place to read the city's origins.

AREA

Dam, central Amsterdam

NEAREST TRANSPORT

Five minutes by tram from Centraal

ALLOW

30–60 minutes

Visit details

Opening hours

The public square is open at all times. The Royal Palace and Nieuwe Kerk have their own changing exhibition hours.

How to get there

From Centraal take trams 2, 4, 12, 13 or 17 to Dam, or walk south along Damrak.

Open in Google Maps

What to know

  • The Royal Palace dominates the square; beside it, the Nieuwe Kerk hosts exhibitions rather than regular worship.
  • The National Monument anchors the eastern side and is the focus of annual remembrance ceremonies.
  • Walk behind the palace into quieter streets or continue south toward the Begijnhof.

Plan your visit

  • The square is crowded and crossed by trams and bicycles, so pause before stepping into a lane.
  • Morning offers the clearest view of the architecture.
  • Treat costumed photo offers and street games as commercial approaches and keep moving if uninterested.

Food & facilities nearby

Food nearby

Leave the square toward the Nine Streets, Spui or Nieuwmarkt for a better range of independent cafés.

Toilets nearby

Public toilets are inside nearby department stores and paid facilities around Damrak; customer toilets require purchase.

Photography & safety

Photography tips

Morning gives clearer architecture. Use the palace façade as an anchor and watch tram wires when composing upward.

Safety tips

Trams, bicycles and crowds cross the space. Ignore street games and unsolicited photo or petition approaches.
