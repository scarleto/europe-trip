🖼️ Rijksmuseum

The national museum of Dutch art and history, centred on Rembrandt, Vermeer and the seventeenth century.

AREA

Museumstraat 1

NEAREST TRANSPORT

Rijksmuseum or Vijzelgracht

ALLOW

2–4 hours

Visit details

Opening hours

Daily 09:00–17:00, including holidays. A start time is required; gardens are generally open 09:00–18:00.

How to get there

Take tram 2 or 12 to Rijksmuseum, tram 1/7/19 to Spiegelgracht, or Metro 52 to Vijzelgracht and walk.

Open in Google Maps

What to know

  • Start with the Gallery of Honour and Night Watch, then choose one historical period or decorative-art collection.
  • The building by Pierre Cuypers and its bicycle passage are part of the Amsterdam experience.
  • Allow time for smaller works rather than moving only between famous paintings.

Plan your visit

  • A timed start is required for everyone.
  • Use the free museum app or map.
  • Visit before 11:00 or after 15:30 for relatively calmer galleries.

Food & facilities nearby

Food nearby

The museum café and RIJKS restaurant are onsite; Museumplein and nearby De Pijp provide alternatives.

Toilets nearby

Accessible visitor toilets are inside the museum; admission is required before 17:00 for the café facilities.

Photography & safety

Photography tips

Personal photography without flash or tripods is generally allowed. The passage beneath the building frames cyclists and architecture.

Safety tips

Use the official timed entry, keep clear of cycle lanes through the passage and store large bags as directed.
