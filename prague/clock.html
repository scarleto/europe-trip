🕰️ Prague Astronomical Clock

A medieval timepiece whose layered dials make more sense when you look beyond the brief hourly procession.

AREA

Old Town Hall

NEAREST TRANSPORT

Staroměstská or Můstek

ALLOW

20–45 minutes

Visit details

Opening hours

The Apostles usually appear hourly 08:00–23:00. Old Town Hall tower and interiors keep separate seasonal daily hours.

How to get there

Take Metro A to Staroměstská or Metro A/B to Můstek and walk to the southwest corner of Old Town Square.

Open in Google Maps

What to know

  • The upper astronomical dial shows multiple kinds of time and the position of celestial bodies; the calendar dial sits below.
  • On the hour, figures move at the windows before the sequence ends with the rooster.
  • The tower and historic halls provide a closer architectural view and a panorama over tiled roofs.

Plan your visit

  • Crowds form directly in front just before the hour; stand farther back for a better overall view.
  • Watch bags when attention is fixed upward.
  • Return early in the morning to study the dial without the crowd.

Food & facilities nearby

Food nearby

Old Town side streets provide better café value than the closest clock-facing terraces.

Toilets nearby

Paid toilets are available within Old Town Hall during opening hours and at nearby commercial venues.

Photography & safety

Photography tips

Stand farther back to include the full tower. Return early to study the dial details without the hourly crowd.

Safety tips

Pickpocket risk rises while everyone looks upward; keep bags closed and leave a path through the gathering.
