🌉 Charles Bridge

The stone bridge between Old Town and Malá Strana is both a landmark and one of Prague's great public spaces.

AREA

Between Old Town and Malá Strana

NEAREST TRANSPORT

Staroměstská or Malostranské náměstí

ALLOW

30–60 minutes

Visit details

Opening hours

The bridge is open at all times. Bridge-tower interiors have separate seasonal daytime hours.

How to get there

Take Metro A to Staroměstská for the east side, or tram 12/15/20/22/23 to Malostranské náměstí for the west side.

Open in Google Maps

What to know

  • The thirty sculptural groups, Gothic bridge towers and shifting castle view reward a slow crossing.
  • Look downstream for the National Theatre and upstream toward quieter Kampa and the Vltava weirs.
  • The Old Town Bridge Tower offers one of the best elevated views along the bridge.

Plan your visit

  • Go at sunrise for space and soft light; sunset is beautiful but extremely busy.
  • Keep to one side if stopping and stay aware of bags in crowds.
  • Walk down to Kampa Island for a different angle at water level.

Food & facilities nearby

Food nearby

Kampa and Malá Strana have cafés close to the west end; Old Town lanes offer more options to the east.

Toilets nearby

Public toilets are available on Kampa and near major squares at either end; many are paid.

Photography & safety

Photography tips

Sunrise offers space and soft light. From Kampa, shoot upward toward the bridge; tower views work well along its length.

Safety tips

Keep to the side when stopping, protect valuables in dense crowds and avoid touching or climbing the statues.
