✈️ Prague Airport

A compact guide to Václav Havel Airport Prague and the public transport connection into the city.

Best general route

Take trolleybus 59 from either terminal to Nádraží Veleslavín, then change to Metro line A for central Prague.

Know your terminal

Terminal 1

Flights arriving from outside the Schengen Area. The public transport stop is directly in front of the arrivals hall.

Terminal 2

Flights arriving from within the Schengen Area. The public transport stop is directly in front of the arrivals hall.

Airport to central Prague

  1. Buy a public transport ticket before boarding. Use a terminal or bus-stop machine, a public transport counter, or the PID Lítačka app. Most airport machines accept contactless cards.
  2. Follow signs for public transport. Stops are immediately outside Terminal 1 and Terminal 2.
  3. Board trolleybus 59. It runs to Nádraží Veleslavín in roughly 15 minutes.
  4. Change to Metro line A. Follow the green A signs and continue toward central stations such as Malostranská, Staroměstská or Můstek.
  5. Keep the ticket valid for the full journey. A correctly timed Prague public transport ticket covers the bus–metro connection.

Other useful routes

Bus 100 + Metro B

Bus 100 reaches Zličín in about 18 minutes, where it connects with yellow Metro line B. Useful for destinations on the western or southern side of line B.

At night

Night buses 907 and 910 link the airport with central districts when the Metro is closed. Confirm the current route in PID Lítačka before leaving the terminal.

Before returning to the airport

  • Check whether your airline uses Terminal 1 or Terminal 2.
  • Plan the live route in PID Lítačka and allow extra time.
  • Use trolleybus 59 from Nádraží Veleslavín and watch the terminal displays before leaving the bus.
