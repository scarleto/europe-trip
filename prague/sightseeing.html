👀 More Prague Sights

Viewpoints, neighbourhoods and parks beyond the core Old Town route.

AREA

Across central Prague

NEAREST TRANSPORT

Multiple tram and Metro stops

ALLOW

Half a day to several days

Visit details

Opening hours

Outdoor streets, parks and viewpoints are always accessible; individual venues set their own hours.

How to get there

Use trams for Malá Strana, Hradčany, Holešovice and Vinohrady; Metro A connects the main historic-centre approaches.

Open in Google Maps

What to know

  • Walk Kampa and Malá Strana below the castle, then seek the city panorama from Letná Park.
  • Vinohrady and Karlín offer elegant streets and everyday city life beyond the historic centre.
  • Vyšehrad combines ramparts, river views and a quieter historical landscape.

Plan your visit

  • Group sights by river side to reduce steep climbs.
  • Use trams uphill and walk downhill.
  • Leave early mornings for the busiest historic places.

Food & facilities nearby

Food nearby

Vinohrady, Karlín and Holešovice offer excellent cafés beyond the most crowded historic streets.

Toilets nearby

Metro stations, museums, shopping centres and paid facilities near major squares are the most dependable options.

Photography & safety

Photography tips

Climb to Letná or Petřín for skyline views, and explore side streets early before tour groups arrive.

Safety tips

Cobbles become slippery when wet. Check menu prices, decline exchange offers and keep belongings secure in bottlenecks.
