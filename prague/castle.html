🏰 Prague Castle

A vast hilltop complex of courtyards, palaces and churches dominated by St Vitus Cathedral.

AREA

Hradčany

NEAREST TRANSPORT

Tram to Pražský hrad or Pohořelec

ALLOW

3–4 hours

Visit details

Opening hours

Castle grounds daily 06:00–22:00. Historic buildings generally 09:00–17:00 in summer, with shorter winter hours.

How to get there

Normally take tram 22 or 23 uphill; 2026 track works may affect Pražský hrad stop, so check PID Lítačka and consider Pohořelec. Metro A to Malostranská requires a climb.

Open in Google Maps

What to know

  • St Vitus Cathedral is the visual centre, but the Old Royal Palace, St George's Basilica and Golden Lane show different eras of the complex.
  • Walk through the courtyards before choosing paid interiors; parts of the grounds can be explored without a circuit ticket.
  • The eastern exit and castle gardens give broad city views before the descent to Malá Strana.

Plan your visit

  • Arrive near opening time and expect security checks.
  • Taking the tram uphill and walking down saves energy on steep streets.
  • Confirm current circuit access and cathedral restrictions on the official site.

Food & facilities nearby

Food nearby

Castle cafés are available inside; Hradčanské náměstí and Nový Svět offer quieter nearby options.

Toilets nearby

Visitor toilets are inside the complex near major courtyards and attractions; some require payment.

Photography & safety

Photography tips

The eastern viewpoints give the city panorama. Courtyards work with wide lenses; follow photography restrictions inside St Vitus.

Safety tips

Expect security screening, cobbles and slopes. Buy circuits only from official offices or the castle website.
