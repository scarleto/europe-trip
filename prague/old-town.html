🏘️ Old Town Square

Prague's historic stage brings together Gothic towers, Baroque façades and centuries of civic life.

AREA

Staroměstské náměstí

NEAREST TRANSPORT

Staroměstská or Můstek

ALLOW

60–90 minutes

Visit details

Opening hours

The square is always open. Old Town Hall interiors and tower generally operate daily with seasonal hours.

How to get there

Take Metro A to Staroměstská or Metro A/B to Můstek, then walk through the pedestrian Old Town streets.

Open in Google Maps

What to know

  • Compare the dark Gothic towers of Týn Church with the pale Baroque front of St Nicholas Church.
  • The Old Town Hall and Astronomical Clock form the southwest corner, but the square's full architecture deserves equal attention.
  • Step into the lanes behind Týn Church for a quieter medieval street pattern.

Plan your visit

  • Arrive early to see the space before tour groups gather.
  • Restaurant terraces directly on the square trade heavily on location; side streets are calmer.
  • Use the clock tower for a high central view if current access allows.

Food & facilities nearby

Food nearby

Move into Dlouhá, Týnská or streets south toward Betlémské náměstí for broader café choice.

Toilets nearby

Paid public toilets are available in or near Old Town Hall and some central commercial venues.

Photography & safety

Photography tips

Arrive early for clear façades. Use the Týn towers, Jan Hus monument and pastel buildings to frame the space.

Safety tips

Watch bags when crowds focus on the clock and check menu prices before choosing a terrace directly on the square.
