✡️ Jewish Quarter

Josefov preserves synagogues, the Old Jewish Cemetery and layered histories of Prague’s Jewish community.

AREA

Josefov, Prague 1

NEAREST TRANSPORT

Staroměstská

ALLOW

2–3 hours

Visit details

Opening hours

May–August 2026: generally 09:00–19:00. Closed Saturdays and Jewish holidays; individual venues may close earlier.

How to get there

Take Metro A to Staroměstská, then walk north into Josefov. The museum route links several separate synagogues and the cemetery.

Open in Google Maps

What to know

  • The Jewish Museum circuit connects several distinct sites rather than one building.
  • Pinkas Synagogue and the Old Jewish Cemetery form a powerful memorial sequence.
  • Spanish Synagogue offers a contrasting Moorish Revival interior and modern historical displays.

Plan your visit

  • Closed Saturdays and Jewish holidays.
  • Check which circuit and venues the ticket includes.
  • Approach the district as living heritage, not merely architecture.

Food & facilities nearby

Food nearby

Pařížská and streets toward Dlouhá have cafés; choose somewhere outside museum queues for a respectful pause.

Toilets nearby

Visitor toilets are available at selected Jewish Museum venues; ask staff when entering the circuit.

Photography & safety

Photography tips

Photography rules differ by synagogue and cemetery. Follow signs, avoid flash and prioritise respectful observation.

Safety tips

This is living heritage and a memorial landscape. Dress appropriately, speak quietly and do not photograph worshippers.
