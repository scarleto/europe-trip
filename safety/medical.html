🩺 Medical Emergencies

Get immediate help first; organise documents and payment afterwards.

Immediate danger

Call 112 for serious illness, breathing difficulty, major injury, loss of consciousness or any rapidly worsening emergency.

Less urgent

A pharmacy can advise on minor problems and direct you to a doctor. Bring medication packaging and explain allergies or existing conditions.

Bring

Carry an EHIC or GHIC if eligible, photo identification and the details of any travel cover. These do not replace emergency treatment decisions.

Verify before relying on this: official guidance .
