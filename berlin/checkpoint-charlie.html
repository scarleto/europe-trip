🚧 Checkpoint Charlie

The best-known former crossing between East and West Berlin, now interpreted through street markers and exhibitions.

AREA

Friedrichstraße at Zimmerstraße

NEAREST TRANSPORT

Kochstraße/Checkpoint Charlie

ALLOW

45–75 minutes

Visit details

Opening hours

The outdoor site is accessible at all times. Nearby private museums and exhibitions set separate hours.

How to get there

Take U-Bahn U6 to Kochstraße/Checkpoint Charlie; the historic crossing is immediately north on Friedrichstraße.

Open in Google Maps

What to know

  • Read the free open-air panels for the border’s history and major Cold War confrontations.
  • Notice the cobbled line marking the former Wall and compare the modern streetscape with historical photographs.
  • Treat the replica guardhouse as a visual marker, not an authentic surviving structure.

Plan your visit

  • Pair it with the nearby Topography of Terror.
  • Go early to see the street with less traffic.
  • Use critical judgement with commercial exhibitions and souvenirs.

Food & facilities nearby

Food nearby

Zimmerstraße and nearby Kreuzberg streets offer cafés; move away from the crossing for less tourist-focused choices.

Toilets nearby

Paid public toilets are available around Friedrichstraße and in customer venues.

Photography & safety

Photography tips

Photograph the historic street markers and open-air panels, not only the replica booth. Early morning reduces traffic.

Safety tips

The replica is not the original checkpoint. Avoid unofficial souvenir sellers and paid photo pressure.
