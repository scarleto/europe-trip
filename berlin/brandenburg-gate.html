🏛️ Brandenburg Gate

Once a symbol of division, the neoclassical gate now stands at the centre of reunited Berlin.

AREA

Pariser Platz

NEAREST TRANSPORT

Brandenburger Tor

ALLOW

30–60 minutes

Visit details

Opening hours

The gate and Pariser Platz are accessible at all times and require no ticket.

How to get there

Take S-Bahn lines S1/S2/S25/S26 or U-Bahn U5 to Brandenburger Tor.

Open in Google Maps

What to know

  • Approach through Unter den Linden for the formal view, then walk through to the open Tiergarten side.
  • The Quadriga above the columns and the proportions of Pariser Platz are clearest from the eastern side.
  • The gate gains meaning from its neighbours: the Reichstag, Holocaust Memorial and remnants of the former border zone.

Plan your visit

  • Visit early or after sunset for fewer tour groups.
  • The area is open at all hours and needs no ticket.
  • Do not sign petitions or engage with aggressive costume-photo solicitations.

Food & facilities nearby

Food nearby

Unter den Linden and the streets toward Friedrichstraße have cafés; Tiergarten kiosks are seasonal.

Toilets nearby

Public toilets are around Tiergarten and major transport stations; cafés provide customer facilities.

Photography & safety

Photography tips

Dawn gives an empty square; after dark the illuminated columns work well from the Tiergarten side.

Safety tips

The area is heavily visited. Ignore aggressive costume-photo, petition or donation approaches.
